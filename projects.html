Below are the highlighting projects I have done at UC Davis.

Classification of Mushrooms: Edible or Poisonous (Python)

This project focuses on developing machine learning and deep learning models to accurately classify mushrooms as edible or poisonous based on their visual characteristics. With the rise in mushroom poisoning incidents, we aim to address the challenges of mushroom classification using a dataset of over 3000 mushroom images. By exploring the potential of these models and considering the limitations of a small dataset, this project contributes to accurate mushroom identification and provides valuable tools for mushroom enthusiasts and field scientists.

Impacts of Vaccination Policy on COVID-19 Cases (R)

In this project, our goal is to assess the impact of government vaccination policies on the spread of Covid-19 across different countries. We analyze time series data from selected countries in the Northern Hemisphere, considering factors such as daily infection rates, testing rates, and vaccination policies. By applying ARIMAX modeling techniques, we aim to build accurate models that can capture the association between vaccination policies and Covid-19 growth. Our findings will provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of vaccination policies in controlling the spread of the virus, allowing policymakers to make informed decisions. Additionally, we address the limitations of our models and discuss future considerations for further analysis.

Abalone Age Prediction Using Three Comparable Models

In this project, we aim to revolutionize the process of estimating the age of abalone by utilizing physical measurements instead of traditional time-consuming methods. With a dataset of 4177 observations and 9 variables, including the response variable "Rings" which corresponds to abalone age, we explore the use of multiple linear regression, ridge regression, and feed-forward neural network models. By addressing challenges such as high correlation between predictor variables, violation of normal assumption, and multicollinearity, we seek to provide a more efficient and accurate approach to estimating abalone age. We aim to uncover an environmentally and economically efficient solution that can potentially transform the abalone industry.

Wheat Classification: Exploring Varietal Characteristics and Relationships

In this research project, our goal is to analyze and classify three common kinds of wheat - Kama, Rosa, and Canadian - based on various features of their kernels. By utilizing data visualization, correlation analysis, and classification techniques, we aim to uncover the distinct characteristics of each wheat variety and establish the relationships between their features. Through methods such as LOOCV, grid search with random forest, and multiclass classification, we will provide valuable insights into the differentiation and classification of these wheat types.
